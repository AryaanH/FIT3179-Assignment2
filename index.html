Visitor Patterns

Number of Visitors by State

New South Wales leads with the highest visitor numbers, topping the list for both years. Tourist numbers across other states remain relatively stable, reflecting a consistent pattern. However, 2024 shows a slight increase in visitors to Victoria and Tasmania, indicating a modest growth in tourism activity in these regions.

Visitor Reasons by State

New South Wales, Victoria, and Queensland lead in visitor numbers, driven mostly by holidays, social visits and business purposes. Smaller states like Tasmania and the Northern Territory see tourism primarily for Holidays. This reflects diverse travel motivations across Australia.

Accommodation Choices

Nights Spent in Different Accommodations by State

Staying with friends or relatives is most common, especially in New South Wales and Queensland. Hotels and motels are also popular, particularly in Victoria and Western Australia. Other options like rented accommodations and non-commercial camping contribute less but show varied preferences across the states. This breakdown highlights diverse accommodation choices in Australian tourism.

Spending Trends

Average Visitor Spending by State

Northern Territory leads in average visitor spending, closely followed by Tasmania, while Victoria has the lowest spending per visitor. In 2024, average spending further declined in Victoria and South Australia, indicating a potential shift in visitor spending behavior. In contrast, Western Australia and the Northern Territory saw significant increases in visitor spending, suggesting heightened economic impact from tourism in these regions.

Spending by Visit Reason

Across Australia, most tourism spending is driven by holidays, followed by business trips. In each state, holiday spending generally dominates; however, the Australian Capital Territory (ACT) stands out, where business trip spending surpasses holiday expenses. In fact, holiday spending in the ACT is even lower than spending on visits to friends and family, highlighting its unique role as a business travel destination.

Domestic Tourist Spending by Category

Accommodation is the largest spending category for domestic tourists in Australia, followed by dining and transportation costs like airfares and fuel. Shopping, groceries, and entertainment also see notable spending, while categories such as organized tours, public transport, and gambling account for much smaller portions. This highlights the main areas where tourists allocate their budgets.
