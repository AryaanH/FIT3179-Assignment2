1. Number of Visitors by State

This map provides an overview of the number of visitors (in millions) for each state in Australia, segmented by year. This information helps in understanding the distribution of tourist footfall across the country. You can toggle between 2023 and 2024 to observe any changes in visitor numbers.

2. Visitor Reasons by State

This bar chart categorizes the reasons for visits to different Australian states, highlighting the purposes for travel such as holidays, business, and visiting friends or relatives. Understanding these reasons can help tailor tourism campaigns and initiatives to attract more visitors in specific segments.

3. Nights Spent in Different Accommodations by State

This chart shows how long visitors stay in different types of accommodations in each state, such as hotels, motels, and private properties. This data offers insights into accommodation preferences, potentially aiding accommodation providers and policymakers in planning and promoting various lodging options.

4. Average Visitor Spending by State

This map visualizes the average amount spent by visitors in each state. By showing spending per visitor, it helps identify which states benefit most economically from each tourist. The data also indicates how spending varies by state, reflecting differences in cost and attractions.

5. Spending by Visit Reason

This pie chart breaks down spending by the purpose of visit, giving an overview of how much tourists spend on holidays, business trips, or visiting friends and family. Understanding these categories helps in assessing which segments contribute most to the tourism economy.

6. Tourist Spending by Category

This bar chart provides insights into the specific spending categories for domestic tourists, ranging from accommodation to transportation and entertainment. This data helps in understanding where the most money is spent, enabling businesses and policymakers to prioritize high-impact areas.
